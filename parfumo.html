✨ Magic in the Bottle: Unveiling the Parfumo Top 100

A luxury fragrance data story by Aadil Khan

Background: This data story visualizes the cleaned and normalized relational SQL database for Parfumo by reproducing the five analytical queries in pandas and visualizing the results in Plotly.

🕵🏽 Key Findings

  • The strongest recent unisex performer by sillage in the filtered top 10 set is Tony Iommi Monkey Special (Xerjoff) with a sillage rating of 8.7.
  • The most common note in the dataset is Vanilla, appearing in 47 perfumes.
  • The concentration with the highest average longevity is Elixir at 8.85.
  • Francis Kurkdjian appears most often among perfumers with at least 3 entries, with 7 perfumes and an average scent rating of 8.09.
  • The most popular accord in the dataset is Spicy, appearing in 73 perfumes.

💨 Sillage Stars

This chart highlights the recent (2015–2024) unisex fragrances that pair strong overall scent reception with the highest sillage. Rather than measuring popularity alone, this chart highlights projection — the fragrances that leave the strongest impression in the air.

Takeaway: The results show which recent perfumes will help generate the most compliments since these are the best-smelling and also likely to be smelled by those around you.

🌸 Note Chemistry

These notes appear most often across the dataset and their average scent rating, but their positions within the fragrance pyramid differ. By stacking top, heart, and base placements, the chart reveals whether a note tends to open brightly, linger in the core, or anchor the drydown.

Takeaway: The most successful perfumes include bergamot and lavender in the opening, lavender and jasmine in the heart, and a variety of options in the drydown, including vanilla, cedarwood, amber, and musk.

🧠 Perfumer Signatures

This bubble chart compares perfumers based on total output, average scent rating, and the number of houses they have worked with. By separating sheer productivity from broader influence, the data highlights which names successfully balance scale, quality, and versatility.

Takeaway: Francis Kurkdjian leads the field with seven of the top 100 fragrances, while Jacques Cavallier-Belletrud holds the highest average scent rating at 8.5. Interestingly, their only accord overlap is in spicy fragrances, suggesting that despite their shared success, they maintain distinct creative styles.

🕰️ Strength in the Spray

While concentration suggests how long a fragrance will last, perceived value ultimately determines whether it feels like a smart purchase. By comparing longevity and value ratings side by side, this analysis reveals which concentration types deliver both durability and consumer satisfaction.

Takeaway: Although higher concentrations naturally lead in longevity, they also come with higher price points and smaller bottle sizes. Consumers actually perceive the greatest value in Eau de Toilette fragrances, while Elixirs rank second in value because their extreme performance helps justify the premium cost despite being the most expensive option.

🎼 Accord Architecture

Accords form the structural identity of a fragrance. This ranking highlights the most common accord families in the dataset and pairs them with the notes most often associated with each one.

Takeaway: The most dominant accords are spicy, sweet, and woody by a considerable margin.
